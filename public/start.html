  • ====== Definitely Not Pictionary ======
    In this game, players will draw the outlandish prompts that Geoff and myself have come up with, using this moderately-functional digital drawing board. The three other players will guess for the correct response while the clock runs down. Geoff will chime in with this noise *Ding* when you get closer to the answer and this noise *Buzz* to indicate incorrect guesses. You will also hear this sound *Hint Boing* with 1:45 left and 0:30 at which point I will give you a hint that I certainly haven’t written ahead of time. Artists, no speaking or writing words on the screen please; symbols and drawings only. Use the left mouse button to draw and the right mouse button to erase. Successful artists will receive 100 points (that's when the audience guesses correctly), and the audience member that guesses correctly will receive 40 points.
  • ====== Pass the Conch ======
    In this game, players will work together to create a heated, passionate debate, of exactly 2 minutes and 30 seconds. Can’t think of anything else to say? You better keep talking because your collective score will decrease for every second of wasted time, babbling, or dead air. Players can call dibs for their preferred side of the argument. Bonus points will be awarded for the winner of the debate, which must be agreed upon by the audience members. In the event of a tie, I will choose the winner. Remember that aside from the bonus you're both getting the same score and your goal is to fill the time with as many coherent ideas as possible.
  • ====== Guess that Growl ======
    In this game, players will guess the name of the animal in the provided audio clip. All players must lock in their answer before we reveal the correct response. I will ask each of you in turn, and that order will be constantly changing. A correct guess earns you 80 points. If you’re not confident in your answer, you can defer your guess until all other players have locked in, for 30 points. Only one person can defer per round. You also have the option to go Double or Negative in this game so keep that in mind if you're feeling confident.
  • ====== Quizball ======
    Our final game of the evening is Quizball. Similar to pong, two players are paired up and you must prevent the ball from getting past you. To move your paddle, hold down the arrow keys and release them to stop. Rounds are best ⅔ and each player will have a chance to play every other player.
    However, there's a catch. Throughout the game I will ask you questions in the format of 'name something is this category', such as “name a character from Sesame Street”. Then I will freeze your paddle. It won't unfreeze until you've answered the question. Prompts will change after each point, and Quizball does have an adjustable speed setting so brace yourselves. 100 points are awarded for the best 2/3 match, and I will award 30 points to the loser if I think you put up a good fight.
  • ====== Pitch the Product ======
    Welcome to our encore event of the evening ladies and gentleman. In this activity you will be provided with a 53 second video clip featuring the product you see on the screen. There’s no audio to accompany the clip, that’s where you come in. Your task is to write a script to accompany the video to create a commercial. Is the product string? Twine? Hemp? You decide and tell us why we should buy it.

    First we’ll watch the clip here together because I want to get your reactions. Then we’ll give you a YouTube link and 10 minutes to watch, pause, and rewind the video as much as you like while creating your masterpiece. After ten minutes we’ll come back to this tab and you’ll each get a chance to pitch your product while we watch the video together. Once we’ve seen all the commercials, each of you will rank them anonymously from best to worst, whatever that means to you. Every first place ranking earns you 80 points, 65, 50, 35 ... and ____ gets you ___ because hey, you tried and we’re all friends here. You are free to rank yourself whatever you like. Garrett and I each have a 50 point bonus that we can award to our favourite commercial.

    Now if you’re not feeling like a creative marketer this evening, that’s totally fine, you may use the duration of footage to roast me instead. Your roast does not even have to be related to the video clip. For 53 seconds the floor is yours, there are no limits. However, choosing the roast option makes you ineligible for the 50 point bonuses Garrett and I will be handing out, and only one of you can use this option. Note that the option is not mandatory, you can all make commercials, it’s up to you guys.
